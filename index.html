Life Insurance FAQs

Here are some of the key benefits of TAL Accelerated Protection Life Insurance:

Death benefit

Your Nominated Beneficiary or the Policy Owner will receive a lump sum payment if you pass away while you have a current Life Insurance policy.

Terminal Illness Benefit

The Life Insured will receive the lump sum Benefit Amount if they’re diagnosed with a Terminal Illness and have less than 12 months to live.

Advanced Payment Benefit

To support your loved ones when they need it most, your beneficiary can get an advance payment of 10% of the Benefit Amount, up to a maximum of $25,000, as soon as we receive the death certificate or medical certificate confirming death.

Repatriation Benefit

In the event you die outside Australia, we’ll increase the maximum amount payable under the Advanced Payment Benefit to 10% of the benefit amount, up to a maximum of $35,000.

You have the flexibility to customise your cover to meet the needs of your family with the following insurance options:

Total and Permanent Disability (TPD) Insurance

Provides you with a lump sum payment of up to $3 million if you have a serious accident or illness that leaves you permanently disabled and unable to work again. This benefit can help with medical, rehab, or living expenses so you can go on making the most of your life. Waiting Periods apply.

Critical Illness insurance

Also known as trauma or recovery insurance, this benefit offers a lump sum payment of up to $2 million if you’re diagnosed and meet the required severity criteria for a critical illness such as cancer, heart attack, or stroke. This benefit can help cover medical expenses, loss of income, and household expenses while you’re undergoing extensive medical treatment. Qualifying Periods apply for some conditions.

Income Protection Insurance

Provides you with a monthly benefit payment of up to 70% of your income, to a maximum of $30,000 per month, if you’re unable to work for a period of time because of illness or injury. It can help replace lost income while you focus on getting back on your feet and returning to work. Waiting Periods apply.

You can also tailor your waiting period (how long before payments begin) and benefit period (how long you can receive the monthly benefit for) to suit your needs and budget.

You’re eligible to apply to get TAL life insurance if you’re:

  • aged between 19 and 74 (age next birthday), and

  • an Australian citizen or permanent resident, or New Zealand resident living permanently in Australia.

There are usually no medical tests required and you can get a non-obligatory quote online within minutes.

Income protection insurance is one type of life insurance. They’re both designed to financially protect you and your family under different circumstances. Here are the key differences:

Income Protection

  • Helps you and your family with expenses if you become sick or injured and are unable to work beyond your Waiting Period.

  • Pays you a monthly benefit to replace part of your income.

  • Replaces lost income for a set period while you focus on getting better so you can return to work.

Life insurance

  • Protects your loved ones financially if you were to pass away or are diagnosed with a terminal illness.

  • Pays your nominated beneficiary or estate a lump sum.
